Indian National Flag

The tricolor is our national flag. All three colors are equally horizontal in size and the saffron color at the top symbolizes courage and sacrifice. The green at the bottom signifies prosperity and fertility. The white in between symbolizes honesty, serenity and purity. The Ashoka Chakra in the middle is dark blue and emphasizes morality and serenity. Venkaiah designed the Indian flag. The first national flag of liberated India was woven in Gudiyatham (Vellore district) in Tamil Nadu. The flag was hoisted by Pandit Jawaharlal Nehru (15.08.1947) at the Red Fort. The flag is currently on public display at the St. George 's Fort Museum in Chennai .The only officially recognized manufacturer of the Indian National Flag is the Karnataka Khadi and Gramyoga Samyukta Sangam in Karnataka. The company operates out of a village called Bangeri in North Karnataka. He was also actively involved in the freedom struggle. The Indian National Flag was flown on May 29, 1953 at Everest, the world's highest peak.
