Mamallapuram

Mamallapuram Beach Temple is the first construction temple in Tamil Nadu. It was built by RajasimhaPallava. The temple is one of the 440 ancient monuments preserved by the Archaeological Survey of Tamil Nadu and stands at a height of 45 feet. The temple was built of black stone from the 8th century (700-728). During the construction of the temple, the place was a functioning port. This place was then ruled by Narasimhavarman II of the Pallava dynasty. The beach temple was declared a World Heritage Site by UNESCO in 1984.
