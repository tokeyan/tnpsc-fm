The JallianwalaBagh Massacre - 1919

The JallianwalaBagh Massacre The colonial government was outraged by the mass protests that took place across the country and the massive spontaneity seen by the people. On April 13, 1919, the most heinous political crimes were committed against an unarmed mob in the JallianwalaBagh area of Amritsar. More than 2,000 people gathered on the battlefield to protest the arrest of Satyapal and SaibuddinKishlu. Michael Dyer was the Deputy Governor of Punjab and General Reginald Dyer was the Army Commander. They both wanted to use their powers to teach the protesters a lesson. There was only one narrow gate to the place where the protesters had gathered. General Dyer ordered machine guns to be fired at machine guns aimed at people trapped there. The death toll could be as high as 379, according to government figures. With the implementation of martial law across Punjab, the people suffered untold hardships. Udham Singh assassinated Michael O'Dwyer on March 30, 1940 at Coxton Arena in London in retaliation for the JallianwalaBagh massacre. Udham Singh was hanged at Bentonville Prison in London. Ravindranath Tagore relinquished his throne immediately after the assassination of JallianwalaBagh. Tagore wrote in a letter of protest sent to the Viceroy on May 31, 1919.
