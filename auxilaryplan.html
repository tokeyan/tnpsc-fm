Axilary Plan

Auxiliary plan was a plan set up by the English East India Company to reduce the rule of the Indian kings and to easily capture their kingdoms. The plan was introduced by Lord Wellesley, Governor of England, who served from 1798 to 1805. According to this plan all kings were to keep the army of the East India Company as a force for the defense of his country and they were not to keep any forces for themselves and they had to pay taxes and money to the companies for this force otherwise they would have to hand over to them a certain place in their territory with fines. Through this plan the British easily captured many parts of India.
