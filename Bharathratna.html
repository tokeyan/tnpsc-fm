Highest Civil Award

The Bharat Ratna Award is given in recognition of outstanding national service. The service covers the fields of art, science, literature, culture, sports (2013) and public service. However, the award was amended in November 2011 to include those in other fields as well. The first definition of the award is a 35 mm diameter gold medal with a sun emblem, Bharat Ratna engraved in Hindi and a floral ring below it, with the state emblem and motto on the back of the medal. . It is also stated that the medal should be attached to a white ribbon and worn around the neck. However no medal seems to have been made in such a design. The following year the design of the medal was changed. According to the 1954 Act, this award cannot be given to immortals. This may be one of the reasons why Mahatma Gandhi was not given this award. However, the 1955 Act made provision for awarding the award to Amars as well. The award has since been presented to ten people after their demise. Although the award is not limited to Indians, it is often given as such. The award was given to Mother Teresa (1980), an Indian citizen born abroad. Apart from him, the award has been given to two non-Indians, Khan Abdul Kapoor Khan (1987) and Nelson Mandela (1990). The award, which was given to Subhash Chandra Bose after his death in 1992, was withdrawn due to legal issues.
