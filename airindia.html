India's First Airline

J.R.D. Tata Airlines was started by 1932. The first service was launched between Karachi and Mumbai. The Tata Group obtained a permit for the aircraft manufacturing plant over the next decade. The then British government also gave permission for the plant to be set up in Pune, but the then government refused permission due to fears that if the Indians gained experience in the aviation sector, it would be detrimental to British companies. Perhaps if permission had been granted then, India's share in aircraft production would have been huge. Subsequently, in 1946, Tata Airlines was renamed Air India. It was also transformed into a public limited company the following year. In 1953, all airlines were nationalized. Therefore, the Tata Group's Air India was also nationalized. JRD Tata led Air India, which became the central government. In 1978, 213 people were died when an Air India plane crashed into the sea. Removed Tata. When Indira Gandhi became the Prime Minister in 1980, JRD again appeared on Air India. Tata was appointed. But at that time he was appointed only as a member of the board of directors and not as a chairman. Ratan Tata was appointed Chairman of Air India in 1986.Air India, a loss-making public sector undertaking, will return to Tata in 2021.
